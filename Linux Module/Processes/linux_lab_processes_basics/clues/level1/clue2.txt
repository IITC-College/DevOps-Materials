WHAT YOU'RE DOING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Sometimes you need to find one specific process in a huge list. Instead of
scrolling through hundreds of lines, you can search for it by name. Your job:
find your bash shell process.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
INSTRUCTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. List all processes. Hint: ps aux.

2. There are too many lines. Let's find only "bash" processes. Filter the
   list to show only lines containing "bash". Hint: ps aux | grep bash.

3. Look at the result. You should see your bash shell (and the grep command).
   Write in my_answers.txt: the PID of one bash process you found. Example:
   "bash PID: 1234"
