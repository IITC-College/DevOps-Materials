WHAT YOU'RE DOING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
When we say a process "eats" CPU or RAM, we mean it uses a lot of that
resource. In the live view you see this as high %CPU or high %MEM. Our dummy
processes (dummy_cpu_eater = high CPU, dummy_ram_eater = high MEM) do this on
purpose. Your job: open the live view, find a process with high CPU or high
memory, and relate what you see to "this process is consuming a lot of that
resource."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
INSTRUCTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Open the live process viewer. Hint: top.

2. Sort by CPU or by memory so the heaviest process is at the top. Find one
   with high %CPU or high %MEM (e.g. dummy_cpu_eater). Hint: P = sort by CPU,
   M = sort by memory inside top.

3. Think: "This process is using a lot of CPU (or RAM) â€“ that's what we mean
   by a process 'eating' that resource."

4. Write in my_answers.txt: which process had the highest CPU or memory when
   you looked? Give its PID and approximate %CPU or %MEM.

5. Exit the viewer. Hint: press q.
