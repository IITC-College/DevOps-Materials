--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT YOU'RE DOING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
When a service is "running," it has at least one process. systemctl status
shows the service state and often the Main PID (the process ID). So: the
service is the "contract"; the process is the actual running program.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
INSTRUCTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Pick one service from the list (e.g. cron, systemd-timesyncd, ssh, or
   another service your instructor suggests). Check its status:
   systemctl status <service_name>
   (Example: systemctl status cron)

2. Look at the output. If the service is "active (running)," you'll usually
   see "Main PID: ..." â€“ that's the process that belongs to this service.
